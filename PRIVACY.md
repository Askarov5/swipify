# Swipify Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** April 2026

Swipify helps you review items in your **photo library** so you can decide what to keep or remove. This policy describes what the app accesses and how it is used.

## What we access

- **Photos and videos** in your library, only after you grant **photo library** permission.
- **Metadata** needed to show your items (for example dates and identifiers the system provides for library items).

We ask for access **only** so you can browse, preview, and make **keep / delete** choices in the app.

## Where processing happens

- **On your device.** Swipify is built so sorting, previews, and your decisions happen **locally**.
- We do **not** upload your photos or videos to our servers for this core flow. There is **no cloud “curator”** that sees your library on your behalf.

## What you control

- You choose whether to **allow photo access** (and can change this in **Settings** on your device).
- **Keep** and **delete** actions are **your** choices. The app applies them through the platform’s APIs (for example marking items as reviewed or removing items you explicitly choose to delete).

## Data we do not sell

We do **not** sell your personal information.

## Children’s privacy

Swipify is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

## Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do.
